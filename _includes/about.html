About CarpentryConnect-Johannesburg 2018

  • What is CarpentryConnect-Johannesburg2018?

    CarpentryConnect events are regional Carpentry Community meetups that take place outside of the main international CarpentryCon meetup. The first CarpentryCon took place in Dublin in May 2018. CarpentryCon is the key community-building and networking event in The Carpentries' annual calendar of activities. The first CarpentryConnect will be held in Johannesburg and will bring together newer and more experienced community members to share knowledge, to network, to develop new skills, and to develop strategies for building strong local communities. The theme of the conference is "Building Bridges". See the A-Z of CarpentryConnect-Johannesburg2018 for specific information.

  • When and where will it be held?


    CarpentryConnect-Johannesburg2018 will be held from 3 - 7 September, 2018, at WITS Professional Development Hub, Johannesburg, South Africa.

  • Who is speaking there?

    We do not plan to have keynote speakers, but will run several practical sessions where participants can choose to learn how to use tools such as R, Python, OpenRefine, Spreadsheets and more at an introductory level, or strengthen their skills in more advanced topics such as image analysis in Python, and more. We will also run a Carpentry instructor training workshop. Invited trainers and instructors include: Allegra Via (Italy), specialised in bioinformatics training; Sarah Brown (USA), electrical engineer with expertise in machine learning; Jez Cope (UK), librarian with computer science and mathematics background; and more...

  • Who is organizing it?

    The event is collaboratively funded and organised by South African Centre for Digital Language Resources (SADiLaR) and the Rural Campuses Connection Project II (RCCPII).

  • How can I contribute?


    Register to attend. We will be looking for co-instructors for the Carpentries workshops as well as helpers. We are also seeking volunteers to support sessions, help with marshalling and registration, as well as many other tasks. Email us if you can help.

  • Will I fit in?

    We are working hard to ensure that everyone feels welcome at CarpentryConnect Johannesburg 2018. Attendees must be prepared to abide by our Code of Conduct. We are providing travel assistance to facilitate attendance from as wide a group as possible.

  • What about my special needs?

    We want to make CarpentryConnect Johannesburg 2018 as accessible as possible. At the ticketing stage, there is plenty of space for you to tell us about any special needs we need to accommodate to make CarpentryConnect work for you.
